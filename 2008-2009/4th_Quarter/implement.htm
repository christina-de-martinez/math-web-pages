Implementing the Distance Formula

Using the distance formula, you can find the distance between any two points on a chart. On a previous webpage, I've given you the formula, but how do you use it?
(if you don't remember what the formula looks like, scroll down.)


Well, if you feel like jogging, and you start out at your house (0,0) and go 4 blocks West (-4,0), and then turn north and go 4 blocks north (-4, -4), how far are you away from your house as a bird flies?
This could be really complicated, OR you could use the distance formula! yay!
All you do is substitute the numbers.
To find the distance between (0,0) and (-4,-4), you need to substitute the numbers in. For example,
d= √ ((0--4)2+(0--4)2)
Then simplify.
d= √ (16+16)

Adding Lengths


When you add a length to another length, it's just like first grade adding.
For example, here is a problem for you:
The quickest way to drive from Chicago to Louisville is to go though Indianapolis. It is 185 miles from Chicago to Indianapolis. It is 114 miles from Indianapolis to Louisville. Along this route, how far is it from Chicago to Louisville?**

To find this answer, all you need to do is add the 185 miles from Chicago to Indianapolis to the 114 miles from Indianapolis to Louisville.

The answer is 299 miles.

 **This was a problem taken from "Transition Mathematics second edition"
